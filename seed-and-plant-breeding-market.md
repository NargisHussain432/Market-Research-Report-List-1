<p><p>The seed and plant breeding market encompasses two primary methods: Normal and Biotechnological. The **Normal Method** involves traditional techniques like cross-breeding and selection to enhance desirable traits such as yield, disease resistance, and adaptability. It relies on natural genetic variation and is widely used for conventional crops. The **Biotechnological Method** employs advanced tools like genetic engineering, CRISPR, and molecular markers to precisely modify plant genomes, enabling faster development of crops with improved traits, such as drought tolerance or enhanced nutrition. Both methods cater to diverse agricultural needs, balancing innovation and tradition.</p></p>
