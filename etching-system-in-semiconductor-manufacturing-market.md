<p><p>The etching system in semiconductor manufacturing consists of two primary types: dry etch systems and wet etch systems. Dry etch systems utilize gases to create plasma for precise material removal, offering high resolution and anisotropic etching suitable for advanced microfabrication. In contrast, wet etch systems involve liquid chemicals to dissolve unwanted materials, providing simpler processes for larger features. Both methods are essential for achieving the desired patterns and structures in semiconductor devices, catering to different manufacturing requirements and technologies.</p></p>
