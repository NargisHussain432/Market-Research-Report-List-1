<p><p>The microplastics sensors market is segmented into three main types: independent sensors, carrier-dependent sensors, and others. Independent sensors operate autonomously to detect microplastics in various environments, providing direct measurements without needing external support. Carrier-dependent sensors, on the other hand, rely on specific mediums or platforms, such as water or air samples, to identify and quantify microplastics. The "others" category encompasses innovative or hybrid technologies that may combine features from both types or offer specialized applications in microplastics detection.</p></p>
