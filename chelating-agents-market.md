<p><p>Chelating agents are chemical compounds that form stable, water-soluble complexes with metal ions, effectively binding and removing them from solutions. They are widely used in industries such as water treatment, detergents, pulp & paper, agriculture, pharmaceuticals, and personal care to control metal ion interference, improve product stability, and enhance performance. Common chelating agents include EDTA, DTPA, NTA, and phosphonates.</p><p>The Chelating Agents Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for clean water, sustainable industrial processes, and eco-friendly products. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14% during the forecast period. Key factors contributing to this growth include stringent environmental regulations, rising awareness of water conservation, and the need for efficient metal ion management in various applications.</p><p>Recent trends in the market include the development of biodegradable and bio-based chelating agents, such as gluconates and citrates, to replace traditional, less environmentally friendly options. Additionally, advancements in nanotechnology and the growing adoption of chelating agents in the pharmaceutical and food industries are creating new opportunities. Asia-Pacific is emerging as a major market due to rapid industrialization, urbanization, and increasing investments in water treatment infrastructure. Overall, the Chelating Agents Market is poised for robust growth, driven by innovation and sustainability trends.</p></p>
