<p><p>Automotive one-way clutches, also known as overrunning clutches, are mechanical devices that allow rotational motion in one direction while preventing it in the opposite direction. As the automotive industry continues to evolve, one-way clutches are expected to play a pivotal role in enabling next-generation vehicle technologies.</p></p>

<p><p>The automotive one-way clutch market includes Sprag-Type, Roller-Type, and other variants. Sprag-Type clutches use wedge-shaped components (sprags) to lock and transmit torque in one direction, offering high load capacity and smooth operation. Roller-Type clutches utilize cylindrical rollers and ramps for one-directional engagement, known for compact design and durability. Other types may include specialized designs for unique applications. These clutches are essential in transmissions, starters, and hybrid systems, ensuring efficient power transfer and preventing reverse rotation.</p></p>
