<p><p>Infectious disease testing using PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) for the In Vitro Diagnostics (IVD) market is classified into two main types: direct detection and indirect detection. Direct detection involves identifying the presence of pathogenic genetic material in a sample, leading to rapid and specific diagnosis. Indirect detection assesses the immune response to pathogens by examining antibodies or other biomarkers, indicating previous or current infections. Both methods play critical roles in disease diagnosis and management, enhancing patient care and treatment outcomes.</p></p>
