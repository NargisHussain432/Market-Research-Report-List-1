<p><p>Low-Density Lipoprotein (LDL) is categorized into various types based on cholesterol content and particle characteristics. LDL-C refers to the cholesterol content within LDL particles, crucial for assessing cardiovascular risk. LDL-P indicates the number of LDL particles, reflecting potential risk more accurately than LDL-C alone. LDL-B focuses on a specific subclass of LDL particles that are smaller and denser, often linked to higher disease risk. Other emerging tests explore additional metrics for a comprehensive understanding of lipid profiles and cardiovascular health.</p></p>
