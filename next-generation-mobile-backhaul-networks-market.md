<p><p>Next-generation mobile backhaul networks consist of two primary types: wired and wireless backhaul. Wired backhaul utilizes fiber optic and copper connections to provide high-capacity, low-latency connectivity, ideal for urban environments and densely populated areas. In contrast, wireless backhaul leverages technologies like microwave and millimeter-wave communications, offering flexibility and rapid deployment in challenging terrains or remote locations. Both types play a crucial role in supporting increased data traffic and enhancing mobile network performance as demand for high-speed connectivity grows.</p></p>
