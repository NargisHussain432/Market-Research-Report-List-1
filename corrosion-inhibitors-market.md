<p><ul><li>Organic</li><li>Inorganic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The corrosion inhibitors market is primarily categorized into organic and inorganic types. Organic inhibitors are typically based on carbon-containing compounds and function by forming protective films on metal surfaces, reducing electrochemical reactions. Examples include amines and phosphates. In contrast, inorganic inhibitors involve mineral-based compounds such as chromates and phosphates, which work by passivating the metal surface to prevent corrosion. Both categories are essential for various industries, including automotive, oil and gas, and construction, ensuring material longevity and reliability.</p></p>
