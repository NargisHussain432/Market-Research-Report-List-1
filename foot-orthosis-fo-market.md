<p><p>The foot orthosis (FO) market is primarily divided into dynamic and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to provide support while allowing for movement, promoting functional mobility and dynamic alignment during activities. They often incorporate materials that adapt to the foot's motion. In contrast, static orthotics aim to maintain a fixed position of the foot, offering stability and alignment for conditions that require immobility. Both types play crucial roles in managing foot-related disorders and enhancing overall foot health.</p></p>
