<p><p>The polyols and polyurethane market comprises two primary types: polyether polyols and polyester polyols. Polyether polyols are derived from the reaction of epoxides with alcohols, known for their flexibility, water resistance, and thermal stability, making them ideal for applications in flexible foams and coatings. Polyester polyols, on the other hand, are produced from the reaction of diols with dicarboxylic acids, offering superior hardness and chemical resistance, making them suitable for rigid foams, elastomers, and automotive applications.</p></p>
