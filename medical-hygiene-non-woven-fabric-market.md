<p><strong>The Medical Hygiene Non Woven Fabric Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Drylaid</li><li>Airlaid</li><li>Wetlaid</li><li>Spunlaid</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The medical hygiene non-woven fabric market consists of four main types: drylaid, airlaid, wetlaid, and spunlaid. Drylaid fabrics are created by bonding fibers with adhesive or mechanical means, resulting in strong materials ideal for disposable hygiene products. Airlaid fabrics involve dispersing fibers in air before bonding, offering a soft texture and high absorbency, suitable for sanitary applications. Wetlaid processes use water to form a web of fibers, yielding highly porous materials, while spunlaid combines thermoplastic fibers, providing strength and flexibility for medical hygiene uses.</p></p>
