<p><p>Neutral plates are pivotal in medical devices, particularly in electrosurgery, where they manage electrical currents. The monopolar market uses a single active electrode to deliver energy through a neutral plate, returning the current to the generator. In contrast, the bipolar market employs two electrodes, allowing for precise control of tissue interaction without the need for a neutral plate. Each market caters to specific surgical needs, offering distinct advantages in procedures, efficiency, and safety in electrical conduction.</p></p>
