<p><p>The biodegradable binder market includes various types, notably ethyl cellulose and alkylene carbonate. Ethyl cellulose is a cellulose ether used for its film-forming properties, providing stability and adhesion in various applications, especially in coatings and pharmaceuticals. Alkylene carbonate serves as a biodegradable solvent and plasticizer, enhancing flexibility and processing of polymer-based materials. Both types contribute to sustainability by offering environmentally friendly alternatives to traditional binders, promoting a reduced ecological footprint in manufacturing and product development.</p></p>
