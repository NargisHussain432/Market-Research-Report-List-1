<p><p>The cellulose-based hemodialysis membrane market includes two main types: regenerated cellulose membranes and modified cellulose membranes. Regenerated cellulose membranes are produced through the dissolving of cellulose and its subsequent regeneration, offering high biocompatibility and filtration efficiency. Modified cellulose membranes, on the other hand, involve chemical alterations to enhance specific properties such as hydrophilicity and permeability, improving performance during dialysis. Both types are critical for effectively removing toxins and waste from blood, ensuring patient safety and comfort in renal therapies.</p></p>
